III. PREMIXER

ELEMENTS

  • Mixer (construction)
  • Motor-reducer with agitator for thick masses with adjustable speed
  • Turbo mixer
  • Duplex space for heating from the central system or via heaters
  • Insulation and insulating jacket
  • Base for easier access to the premixer when adding raw materials
  • Piping with automatic valves from the premixer to the ball mill
  • Temperature maintenance equipment for water or mass
  • Process control system
  • Technical documentation with operating and maintenance instructions

TECHNICAL DESCRIPTION

The tank for mass preparation is designed to combine all components that make up chocolate (vegetable fat, cocoa, sugar, cocoa butter, etc.) before feeding them into the homogenizing and grinding mill.

The melted vegetable fat or cocoa butter is transferred to the premixer, while other components are directly added into the premixer, where specially designed agitators are used to homogenize the mass.

During this time, a pump continuously recirculates the mass, enabling it to blend in the shortest period. The prepared mass is then transported via a ball mill through a pipeline and pump system.

TECHNICAL SPECIFICATIONS

The entire construction of the premixer is made of stainless steel, which is suitable for such applications in the confectionery industry. The piping and accompanying fittings are also made of stainless steel.

  • Working medium: chocolate (cream) mass

Conching is a modern process used in chocolate preparation. The characteristic taste, smell, and texture of chocolate are achieved during this phase.

Sugar and cocoa particles are smoothed during conching, but finer grinding is performed in the ball mill. During conching, the sharp taste of fresh cocoa gradually disappears, while acidity, bitterness, and moisture are reduced, revealing the full distinctive flavor of chocolate.

At the same time, a layer of cocoa butter forms around the tiny sugar and cocoa particles. Conching can also be performed in the ball mill itself, but since that system is not ideal for evaporation, a fan is installed on the conche.

A rotating disk spins above the surface of the mass and creates a thin layer of material which, in combination with ventilation, ensures efficient removal of moisture and acids from the chocolate.

The ball mill alone offers limited options for defining the chocolate's flavor. It is important to consider the role of conching in the chocolate production process: beyond imparting the chocolate’s unique taste, this process must ensure that the mass is homogeneous and has excellent rheological properties during processing and in the final product’s taste.
